About me

My name is Jessica Freeman. I graduated from the University of Georgia with an Anthropology degree focused in Archaeology. We used GIS for mapping archaeological sites, analyzing field conditions and likelihood of recovering archaeological evidence, and georeferencing of historic documents and aerial imagery.

I am currently a Technical Project Specialist I for ITS Information Services Geospatial group at the University of Kentucky. My position focuses on project management, printable map development, web mapping solutions, process development, and various office support functions. My department works closely with other campus departments to maintain accurate datasets and standards for UK facilities. The favorite part of my job is research and design of GIS solutions for our clients. This can involve development of printable maps, interactive web apps, Collector environments, Survey123 forms, and integrating these products to work seamlessly with each other. I am enrolled in the UK New Maps Plus Program to increase my knowledge of custom web map development and coding capabilities for my current position as a Technical Project Specialist.
